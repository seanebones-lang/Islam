What is Islam?

An Introduction to the Religion of Peace

Islam: The Religion of Peace

Islam is a monotheistic religion that teaches there is only one God (Allah in Arabic) and that Muhammad (peace be upon him) is His final messenger. The word "Islam" comes from the Arabic root s-l-m, which means peace, and also means submission to the will of Allah.

"لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ مُحَمَّدٌ رَّسُولُ اللَّهِ"
"There is no god but Allah, and Muhammad is His messenger" - The Shahada (Declaration of Faith)

The Five Pillars of Islam

1. Shahada (Declaration of Faith)

The testimony that there is no god but Allah and Muhammad is His messenger

2. Salah (Prayer)

Five daily prayers performed at specific times throughout the day

3. Zakat (Charity)

Giving a portion of one's wealth to help those in need

4. Sawm (Fasting)

Fasting during the holy month of Ramadan from dawn to sunset

5. Hajj (Pilgrimage)

Pilgrimage to Mecca at least once in a lifetime if able

Core Beliefs

Tawhid (Oneness of God)

The fundamental belief that Allah is one, unique, and has no partners

Prophets

Belief in all prophets sent by Allah, including Abraham, Moses, Jesus, and Muhammad

Holy Books

Belief in divine scriptures, with the Quran being the final revelation

Day of Judgment

Belief in life after death and accountability for one's actions
