Essential Islamic Phrases

1
السَّلاَمُ عَلَيْكُمْ

As-salamu alaykum

"Peace be upon you" - Islamic greeting
2
وَعَلَيْكُمُ السَّلاَمُ

Wa alaykumu s-salam

"And upon you peace" - Response to greeting
3
بِسْمِ اللَّهِ

Bismillah

"In the name of Allah" - Before starting anything
4
الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ

Alhamdulillah

"All praise to Allah" - Expression of gratitude
5
سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ

SubhanAllah

"Glory to Allah" - Expression of amazement
6
اللَّهُ أَكْبَرُ

Allahu Akbar

"Allah is Greatest" - Expression of Allah's greatness

Prayer Arabic

Takbir (Opening Prayer)

اللَّهُ أَكْبَرُ

Allahu Akbar - "Allah is Greatest"

Al-Fatihah (The Opening)

الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ رَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ

Alhamdu lillahi rabbil alameen - "Praise to Allah, Lord of all worlds"

Ruku (Bowing)

سُبْحَانَ رَبِّيَ الْعَظِيمِ

Subhana rabbiyal azeem - "Glory to my Lord, the Great"

Sujud (Prostration)

سُبْحَانَ رَبِّيَ الْأَعْلَى

Subhana rabbiyal a'la - "Glory to my Lord, the Most High"

Pronunciation Guide

Master the correct pronunciation of Arabic letters and sounds with our comprehensive guide.

Difficult Sounds for English Speakers

ع
Ayn (ع)
Deep throat sound - like clearing your throat
💡 Tip: Practice with "Allah" - the 'A' sound comes from deep in throat
غ
Ghayn (غ)
Similar to French 'r' or Spanish 'j'
💡 Tip: Like gargling water but with air
ح
Ha (ح)
Strong 'h' sound from throat
💡 Tip: Breathe out with mouth open, like fogging a mirror
خ
Kha (خ)
Like Scottish 'ch' in 'loch'
💡 Tip: Like clearing throat with 'k' sound

Common Pronunciation Mistakes

❌

Confusing 'a' and 'aa' sounds

Many learners pronounce 'a' (fatha) and 'aa' (alif) the same way

Correct: باب (baab) - Door بَت (bat) - Girl
❌

Not distinguishing emphatic letters

Letters like ص، ض، ط، ظ have a deeper, more emphatic sound

Correct: صلاة (salah) - Prayer ضمان (daman) - Guarantee
