What is Sunnah?

The Sunnah refers to the practices, actions, and way of life of Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him). It serves as the second source of Islamic guidance after the Quran and provides practical examples of how to implement Islamic teachings in daily life.

Categories of Sunnah

Ibadah (Worship)

How the Prophet performed prayers, fasting, charity, and other acts of worship

Mu'amalat (Social Relations)

How he interacted with family, friends, neighbors, and the community

Adab (Etiquette)

Manners, behavior, and daily habits of the Prophet

Ahkam (Legal Rulings)

Judicial decisions and legal precedents established by the Prophet

Prophet's Character Traits

Truthfulness (Sidq)

He was known as "Al-Amin" (the trustworthy) even before prophethood. Even his enemies testified to his truthfulness. (Sahih Bukhari 3)

Mercy (Rahma)

Allah described him as "a mercy to all the worlds" (Quran 21:107). He showed mercy to children, animals, and even enemies. (Sahih Muslim 2319)

Patience (Sabr)

He endured 13 years of persecution in Mecca with remarkable patience, never seeking revenge. (Sahih Bukhari 3231)

Forgiveness (Afw)

He forgave even his worst enemies when he had the power to retaliate, including those who persecuted him for years. (Sahih Muslim 1792)

Humility (Tawadu')

He would sit on the ground, mend his own clothes, and serve his family. (Sahih Bukhari 5374)

Generosity (Karam)

He was the most generous of people, especially during Ramadan. (Sahih Bukhari 6)

Justice (Adl)

He established justice for all, regardless of social status, wealth, or family connections. (Sahih Muslim 1718)

Modesty (Haya)

He was more modest than a virgin in her chamber. (Sahih Bukhari 6119)

Prophet's Physical Appearance & Mannerisms

Physical Description

He was of medium height, neither very tall nor short. His complexion was fair with a reddish tinge. His hair was neither curly nor straight, and he had a broad forehead. (Sahih Bukhari 3549)

Walking Style

He walked with firm steps, as if descending from a high place. When he turned, he would turn his whole body. (Sahih Muslim 2339)

Smile & Laughter

He had a beautiful smile and would laugh rarely, but when he did, his teeth would show like hailstones. (Sahih Bukhari 245)

Hand Gestures

He would point with his whole hand when speaking, and when he was surprised, he would turn his palm upward. (Sahih Muslim 2339)

Etiquette & Manners (Adab)

Greeting Others

He would greet everyone, including children and slaves. He was the first to greet others and would shake hands with both hands. (Sahih Bukhari 6243)

Speaking

He spoke clearly and distinctly, repeating important points three times. He would pause between sentences to ensure understanding. (Sahih Bukhari 95)

Listening

He would give his full attention to whoever was speaking, never interrupting. He would face the speaker completely. (Sahih Muslim 2494)

Visiting the Sick

He would visit the sick regularly, place his hand on their forehead, and make supplications for their recovery. (Sahih Bukhari 5675)

Attending Funerals

He would attend funerals of both Muslims and non-Muslims, showing respect for all humanity. (Sahih Bukhari 1312)

Respect for Elders

He would stand up for elderly people, give them priority in seating, and speak to them with utmost respect. (Sahih Bukhari 2442)

Family Life & Relationships

Treatment of Wives

He would help with household chores, mend his own clothes, and treat his wives with kindness and respect. (Sahih Bukhari 676)

Children's Rights

He would play with children, carry them on his shoulders, and never hit a child. He emphasized the importance of treating children with love. (Sahih Bukhari 5995)

Neighbors

He emphasized the rights of neighbors so much that it was thought they might inherit from each other. He would visit and help neighbors in need. (Sahih Bukhari 6014)

Servants & Workers

He would eat with his servants, share the same food, and never treated them as inferior. He said, "They are your brothers whom Allah has put under your authority." (Sahih Bukhari 30)

Business & Social Interactions

Honest Trading

He was known for his honesty in business even before prophethood. He never cheated, lied, or broke promises in trade. (Sahih Bukhari 3)

Fair Dealing

He would weigh goods himself to ensure fairness and would give extra when selling, never taking advantage of others. (Sahih Muslim 1601)

Debt Management

He would pay debts promptly and would help others pay their debts if they were unable. He never delayed payment. (Sahih Bukhari 2391)

Charity & Generosity

He would give charity regularly and would never refuse anyone who asked for help. He was especially generous during Ramadan. (Sahih Bukhari 6)

Health & Hygiene Practices

Oral Hygiene

He emphasized the use of miswak (tooth stick) and said, "If it were not difficult for my Ummah, I would have commanded them to use the miswak before every prayer." (Sahih Bukhari 887)

Regular Bathing

He would take a bath every Friday and encouraged regular bathing. He said, "It is the right of Allah upon every Muslim to take a bath at least once every seven days." (Sahih Bukhari 898)

Nail Care

He would trim his nails regularly and advised others to do the same. He would cut his nails on Fridays as part of his weekly grooming routine. (Sunan Abu Dawood 4201)

Hair Care

He would comb his hair regularly and apply oil to it. He said, "Whoever has hair should honor it." (Sunan Abu Dawood 4163)

Clean Clothing

He always wore clean clothes and emphasized cleanliness. He said, "Cleanliness is half of faith." (Sahih Muslim 223)

Moderate Eating

He would eat moderately and never overeat. He said, "The son of Adam does not fill any vessel worse than his stomach." (Sunan Tirmidhi 2380)
