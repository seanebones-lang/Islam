Abasa (He Frowned)

Meccan 42 Verses
بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ
Bismillah-ir-Rahman-ir-Raheem

Abasa wa tawalla
He frowned and turned away
2
أَن جَاءَهُ الْأَعْمَىٰ
An ja'ahu-l-a'ma
Because there came to him the blind man, [interrupting].
3
وَمَا يُدْرِيكَ لَعَلَّهُ يَزَّكَّىٰ
Wa ma yudreeka la'allahu yazzakka
But what would make you perceive, [O Muhammad], that perhaps he might be purified
4
أَوْ يَذَّكَّرُ فَتَنفَعَهُ الذِّكْرَىٰ
Aw yadhakkaru fa tanfa'ahu-dh-dhikra
Or be reminded and the remembrance would benefit him?
5

Amma man istaghna
As for he who thinks himself self-sufficient,
6
فَأَنتَ لَهُ تَصَدَّىٰ
Fa anta lahu tasadda
To him you give attention.
7
وَمَا عَلَيْكَ أَلَّا يَزَّكَّىٰ
Wa ma 'alayka alla yazzakka
And not upon you [is any blame] if he will not be purified.
8
وَأَمَّا مَن جَاءَكَ يَسْعَىٰ
Wa amma man ja'aka yas'a
But as for he who came to you striving [for knowledge]
9
وَهُوَ يَخْشَىٰ
Wa huwa yakhsha
While he fears [Allah],
10
فَأَنتَ عَنْهُ تَلَهَّىٰ
Fa anta 'anhu talahha
From him you are distracted.

Tafsir (Commentary)

Context: This Surah was revealed when the Prophet (peace be upon him) was speaking to some of the Quraysh leaders, and a blind man named Abdullah ibn Umm Maktum came to him seeking knowledge. The Prophet frowned and turned away from him to continue his conversation with the wealthy leaders.

Key Themes:

  • Allah's guidance is not based on worldly status or wealth
  • The importance of treating all seekers of knowledge equally
  • Reminder that Allah knows who will benefit from guidance
  • Warning against prioritizing worldly matters over spiritual guidance

Lessons: This Surah teaches us that knowledge and guidance should be accessible to everyone, regardless of their social status or appearance. It reminds us that Allah's mercy and guidance are not limited to the wealthy or powerful, but are available to all who sincerely seek them.

سُورَةُ الْقَدْر

Al-Qadr (The Power)

Meccan 5 Verses
بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ
Bismillah-ir-Rahman-ir-Raheem
In the name of Allah, the Entirely Merciful, the Especially Merciful.
1
إِنَّا أَنزَلْنَاهُ فِي لَيْلَةِ الْقَدْرِ
Inna anzalnahu fi laylati-l-qadr
Indeed, We sent the Qur'an down during the Night of Decree.
2
وَمَا أَدْرَاكَ مَا لَيْلَةُ الْقَدْرِ
Wa ma adraka ma laylatu-l-qadr
And what can make you know what is the Night of Decree?
3
لَيْلَةُ الْقَدْرِ خَيْرٌ مِّنْ أَلْفِ شَهْرٍ
Laylatu-l-qadri khayrun min alfi shahr
The Night of Decree is better than a thousand months.
4
تَنَزَّلُ الْمَلَائِكَةُ وَالرُّوحُ فِيهَا بِإِذْنِ رَبِّهِم مِّن كُلِّ أَمْرٍ
Tanazzalu-l-mala'ikatu wa-r-ruhu fiha bi-idhni rabbihim min kulli amr
The angels and the Spirit descend therein by permission of their Lord for every matter.
5
سَلَامٌ هِيَ حَتَّىٰ مَطْلَعِ الْفَجْرِ
Salamun hiya hatta matla'i-l-fajr
Peace it is until the emergence of dawn.

Tafsir (Commentary)

Context: This Surah was revealed in Mecca and describes the Night of Decree (Laylat al-Qadr), which is better than a thousand months. It is believed to be one of the last ten nights of Ramadan.

Key Themes:

  • The revelation of the Quran during Laylat al-Qadr
  • The immense value and blessing of this special night
  • Angels and the Spirit descend with Allah's permission
  • Peace and tranquility characterize this blessed night

Significance: Laylat al-Qadr is considered the most blessed night of the year. Worship during this night is equivalent to worshiping for more than 83 years. Muslims are encouraged to seek this night during the last ten nights of Ramadan through increased prayer, recitation of the Quran, and supplication.

Lessons: This Surah emphasizes the importance of seeking Allah's mercy and blessings during special times. It reminds us that Allah's rewards are not limited by time, and sincere worship can yield immense spiritual benefits.

سُورَةُ الْعَصْر

Al-Asr (The Time)

Meccan 3 Verses
بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ
Bismillah-ir-Rahman-ir-Raheem
In the name of Allah, the Entirely Merciful, the Especially Merciful.
1
وَالْعَصْرِ
Wa-l-'asr
By time,
2
إِنَّ الْإِنسَانَ لَفِي خُسْرٍ
Inna-l-insana lafi khusr
Indeed, mankind is in loss,
3
إِلَّا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَعَمِلُوا الصَّالِحَاتِ وَتَوَاصَوْا بِالْحَقِّ وَتَوَاصَوْا بِالصَّبْرِ
Illa-l-ladhina amanu wa 'amilu-s-salihati wa tawasaw bi-l-haqqi wa tawasaw bi-s-sabr
Except for those who have believed and done righteous deeds and advised each other to truth and advised each other to patience.

Tafsir (Commentary)

Context: This short but profound Surah was revealed in Mecca. Despite being only three verses, it contains a complete summary of the path to success in this life and the next.

Key Themes:

  • Time as a witness to human condition
  • Humanity's general state of loss
  • Four essential qualities for success: faith, good deeds, mutual advice for truth, and mutual advice for patience

Significance: Imam Shafi'i said that if Allah had revealed only this Surah, it would have been sufficient for guidance. It provides a complete formula for success that includes both individual and communal responsibilities.

Lessons: This Surah teaches us that success requires four elements: 1) Faith in Allah, 2) Righteous actions, 3) Encouraging others to truth, and 4) Encouraging others to patience. It emphasizes that true success is not individual but communal, requiring mutual support and encouragement in righteousness.

سُورَةُ غَافِر

Ghafir (The Forgiver)

Meccan 85 Verses
بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ
Bismillah-ir-Rahman-ir-Raheem
In the name of Allah, the Entirely Merciful, the Especially Merciful.
1
حم
Ha Meem
Ha, Meem.
These are the mysterious letters (Muqatta'at) that appear at the beginning of certain Surahs. Their exact meaning is known only to Allah, but they serve as a reminder of the miraculous nature of the Quran.
2
تَنزِيلُ الْكِتَابِ مِنَ اللَّهِ الْعَزِيزِ الْعَلِيمِ
Tanzilu-l-kitabi mina Allahi-l-'azizi-l-'aleem
[This is] the revelation of the Book from Allah, the Exalted in Might, the Knowing,
The Quran is revealed by Allah, who is All-Powerful and All-Knowing. This establishes the divine origin and authority of the message.
3
غَافِرِ الذَّنبِ وَقَابِلِ التَّوْبِ شَدِيدِ الْعِقَابِ ذِي الطَّوْلِ ۖ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ ۖ إِلَيْهِ الْمَصِيرُ
Ghafiri-dh-dhanbi wa qabili-t-tawb shadidi-l-'iqab dhi-t-tawl la ilaha illa huwa ilayhi-l-maseer
The forgiver of sin, acceptor of repentance, severe in punishment, owner of abundance. There is no deity except Him; to Him is the destination.
Allah is described with four key attributes: He forgives sins, accepts repentance, punishes severely when necessary, and is the source of all abundance. The ultimate destination is to Him alone.
7
الَّذِينَ يَحْمِلُونَ الْعَرْشَ وَمَنْ حَوْلَهُ يُسَبِّحُونَ بِحَمْدِ رَبِّهِمْ وَيُؤْمِنُونَ بِهِ وَيَسْتَغْفِرُونَ لِلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا رَبَّنَا وَسِعْتَ كُلَّ شَيْءٍ رَّحْمَةً وَعِلْمًا فَاغْفِرْ لِلَّذِينَ تَابُوا وَاتَّبَعُوا سَبِيلَكَ وَقِهِمْ عَذَابَ الْجَحِيمِ
Alladhina yahmiluna-l-'arsha wa man hawlahu yusabbihuna bihamdi rabbihim wa yu'minuna bihi wa yastaghfiruna li-lladhina amanu rabbana wasi'ta kulla shay'in rahmatan wa 'ilman fa-ghfir li-lladhina tabu wa-ttaba'u sabilaka wa qihim 'adhaba-l-jahimi
Those [angels] who carry the Throne and those around it exalt [Allah] with praise of their Lord and believe in Him and ask forgiveness for those who have believed, [saying], "Our Lord, You have encompassed all things in mercy and knowledge, so forgive those who have repented and followed Your way and protect them from the punishment of Hellfire."
The angels who carry the Throne and those around it continuously praise Allah and intercede for believers, asking for their forgiveness and protection from Hellfire.

Qaf

Meccan 45 Verses
بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ
Bismillah-ir-Rahman-ir-Raheem
In the name of Allah, the Entirely Merciful, the Especially Merciful.
1
ق ۚ وَالْقُرْآنِ الْمَجِيدِ
Qaf wa-l-Qur'ani-l-majeed
Qaf. By the honored Qur'an...
Qaf is one of the mysterious letters (Muqatta'at) that appear at the beginning of certain Surahs. The Quran is described as "majeed" (honored/glorious), emphasizing its exalted status.
2
بَلْ عَجِبُوا أَن جَاءَهُم مَّنذِرٌ مِّنْهُمْ فَقَالَ الْكَافِرُونَ هَٰذَا شَيْءٌ عَجِيبٌ
Bal 'ajibu an ja'ahum mundhirun minhum fa-qala-l-kafiruna hadha shay'un 'ajib
But they wonder that there has come to them a warner from among themselves, and the disbelievers say, "This is an amazing thing."
The disbelievers are surprised that a warner (Prophet Muhammad) has come from among them, considering it strange that one of their own people would claim to be a prophet.
3
أَإِذَا مِتْنَا وَكُنَّا تُرَابًا ۖ ذَٰلِكَ رَجْعٌ بَعِيدٌ
A-idha mitna wa kunna turaban dhalika raj'un ba'eed
"When we have died and have become dust, [we will return to life]? That is a distant return."
The disbelievers express doubt about the resurrection, questioning how they could be brought back to life after becoming dust, considering it impossible.
4
قَدْ عَلِمْنَا مَا تَنقُصُ الْأَرْضُ مِنْهُمْ ۖ وَعِندَنَا كِتَابٌ حَفِيظٌ
Qad 'alimna ma tanqusu-l-ardu minhum wa 'indana kitabun hafeeth
We know what the earth diminishes of them, and with Us is a preserving record.
Allah knows exactly what the earth consumes of their bodies, and He has a complete record of everything, so resurrection is not difficult for Him.
5
بَلْ كَذَّبُوا بِالْحَقِّ لَمَّا جَاءَهُمْ فَهُمْ فِي أَمْرٍ مَّرِيجٍ
Bal kaththabu bi-l-haqqi lamma ja'ahum fa-hum fi amrin mareej
But they denied the truth when it came to them, so they are in a confused condition.
The disbelievers rejected the truth when it came to them, leaving them in a state of confusion and uncertainty about their beliefs.

سُورَةُ الْوَاقِعَة

Al-Waqi'ah (The Event)

Meccan 96 Verses
بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ
Bismillah-ir-Rahman-ir-Raheem
In the name of Allah, the Entirely Merciful, the Especially Merciful.
1
إِذَا وَقَعَتِ الْوَاقِعَةُ
Idha waqa'ati-l-waqi'ah
When the Occurrence occurs,
This refers to the Day of Judgment, which is called "Al-Waqi'ah" (The Event) because it is the most significant event that will ever occur.
2
لَيْسَ لِوَقْعَتِهَا كَاذِبَةٌ
Laysa li-waq'atiha kathibah
There is, at its occurrence, no denial.
When the Day of Judgment occurs, no one will be able to deny it or claim it is false, as it will be clearly evident to all.
3
خَافِضَةٌ رَّافِعَةٌ
Khafidatun rafi'ah
It will bring down [some] and raise up [others].
The Day of Judgment will humble the arrogant and elevate the humble believers, completely reversing the worldly order.
4
إِذَا رُجَّتِ الْأَرْضُ رَجًّا
Idha rujjati-l-ardu rajjan
When the earth is shaken with a [final] earthquake
The earth will be violently shaken, causing massive destruction and upheaval as part of the signs of the Day of Judgment.
5
وَبُسَّتِ الْجِبَالُ بَسًّا
Wa bussati-l-jibalu bassan
And the mountains are crumbled to pieces
The mighty mountains will be completely pulverized and scattered like dust, showing the immense power of Allah on that day.
6
فَكَانَتْ هَبَاءً مُّنبَثًّا
Fa-kanat haba'an munbaththan
So they become scattered dust
The mountains will be reduced to fine dust particles scattered everywhere, demonstrating the complete transformation of the earth's landscape.

[Containing] the fire full of fuel,

إِذْ هُمْ عَلَيْهَا قُعُودٌ
Idh hum 'alayha qu'ud
When they were sitting near it
7
وَهُمْ عَلَىٰ مَا يَفْعَلُونَ بِالْمُؤْمِنِينَ شُهُودٌ
Wa hum 'ala ma yaf'aluna bi-l-mu'minina shuhud
And they, to what they were doing against the believers, were witnesses.
8
وَمَا نَقَمُوا مِنْهُمْ إِلَّا أَن يُؤْمِنُوا بِاللَّهِ الْعَزِيزِ الْحَمِيدِ
Wa ma naqamu minhum illa an yu'minu bi-llahi-l-'azizi-l-hamid
And they resented them not except because they believed in Allah, the Exalted in Might, the Praiseworthy,
9
الَّذِي لَهُ مُلْكُ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ ۚ وَاللَّهُ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ شَهِيدٌ
Alladhi lahu mulku-s-samawati wa-l-ard wa-llahu 'ala kulli shay'in shahid
To whom belongs the dominion of the heavens and the earth. And Allah, over all things, is Witness.
10
إِنَّ الَّذِينَ فَتَنُوا الْمُؤْمِنِينَ وَالْمُؤْمِنَاتِ ثُمَّ لَمْ يَتُوبُوا فَلَهُمْ عَذَابُ جَهَنَّمَ وَلَهُمْ عَذَابُ الْحَرِيقِ
Inna-l-ladhina fatanu-l-mu'minina wa-l-mu'minati thumma lam yatubu fa-lahum 'adhabu jahannam wa lahum 'adhabu-l-hariq
Indeed, those who have tortured the believing men and believing women and then have not repented will have the punishment of Hell, and they will have the punishment of the Burning Fire.
11
إِنَّ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَعَمِلُوا الصَّالِحَاتِ لَهُمْ جَنَّاتٌ تَجْرِي مِن تَحْتِهَا الْأَنْهَارُ ۚ ذَٰلِكَ الْفَوْزُ الْكَبِيرُ
Inna-l-ladhina amanu wa 'amilu-s-salihati lahum jannatun tajri min tahtiha-l-anhar dhalika-l-fawzu-l-kabir
Indeed, those who believe and do righteous deeds will have gardens beneath which rivers flow. That is the great attainment.
12
إِنَّ بَطْشَ رَبِّكَ لَشَدِيدٌ
Inna batsha rabbika la-shadid
Indeed, the assault [and seizure] of your Lord is severe.
13
إِنَّهُ هُوَ يُبْدِئُ وَيُعِيدُ
Innahu huwa yubdi'u wa yu'id
Indeed, it is He who originates [creation] and repeats.
14
وَهُوَ الْغَفُورُ الْوَدُودُ
Wa huwa-l-ghafuru-l-wadud
And He is the Forgiving and Affectionate,
15
ذُو الْعَرْشِ الْمَجِيدُ
Dhu-l-'arshi-l-majid
Honorable Owner of the Throne,
16
فَعَّالٌ لِّمَا يُرِيدُ
Fa''alun li-ma yurid
Effecter of what He intends.
17
هَلْ أَتَاكَ حَدِيثُ الْجُنُودِ
Hal ataka hadithu-l-junud
Has there reached you the story of the soldiers -
18
فِرْعَوْنَ وَثَمُودَ
Fir'awna wa thamud
[Those of] Pharaoh and Thamud?
19
بَلِ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا فِي تَكْذِيبٍ
Bali-l-ladhina kafaru fi takdhib
But those who disbelieve are in [persistent] denial,
20
وَاللَّهُ مِن وَرَائِهِم مُّحِيطٌ
Wa-llahu min wara'ihim muhit
While Allah encompasses them from behind.
21
بَلْ هُوَ قُرْآنٌ مَّجِيدٌ
Bal huwa qur'anun majid
But this is an honored Qur'an
22
فِي لَوْحٍ مَّحْفُوظٍ
Fi lawhin mahfuz
[Inscribed] in a Preserved Slate.

سُورَةُ الفَجْر

Al-Fajr (The Dawn)

Meccan 30 Verses
بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ
Bismillah-ir-Rahman-ir-Raheem
In the name of Allah, the Entirely Merciful, the Especially Merciful.
1
وَالْفَجْرِ
Wa-l-fajr
By the dawn
2
وَلَيَالٍ عَشْرٍ
Wa layalin 'ashr
And [by] ten nights
3
وَالشَّفْعِ وَالْوَتْرِ
Wa-sh-shaf'i wa-l-watr
And [by] the even [number] and the odd
4
وَاللَّيْلِ إِذَا يَسْرِ
Wa-l-layli idha yasr
And [by] the night when it passes,
5
هَلْ فِي ذَٰلِكَ قَسَمٌ لِّذِي حِجْرٍ
Hal fi dhalika qasamun li-dhi hijr
Is there [not] in [all] that an oath [sufficient] for one of perception?
6
أَلَمْ تَرَ كَيْفَ فَعَلَ رَبُّكَ بِعَادٍ
Alam tara kayfa fa'ala rabbuka bi-'ad
Have you not considered how your Lord dealt with 'Aad -
7
إِرَمَ ذَاتِ الْعِمَادِ
Irama dhati-l-'imad
[With] Iram - who had lofty pillars,
8
الَّتِي لَمْ يُخْلَقْ مِثْلُهَا فِي الْبِلَادِ
Allati lam yukhlaq mithluha fi-l-bilad
The likes of whom had never been created in the land?
9
وَثَمُودَ الَّذِينَ جَابُوا الصَّخْرَ بِالْوَادِ
Wa thamuda-l-ladhina jabu-s-sakhra bi-l-wad
And [with] Thamud, who carved out the rocks in the valley?
10
وَفِرْعَوْنَ ذِي الْأَوْتَادِ
Wa fir'awna dhi-l-awtad
And [with] Pharaoh, owner of the stakes?
11
الَّذِينَ طَغَوْا فِي الْبِلَادِ
Alladhina taghaw fi-l-bilad
[All of] whom oppressed within the lands
12
فَأَكْثَرُوا فِيهَا الْفَسَادَ
Fa-aktharu fiha-l-fasad
And increased therein the corruption.
13
فَصَبَّ عَلَيْهِمْ رَبُّكَ سَوْطَ عَذَابٍ
Fa-sabba 'alayhim rabbuka sawta 'adhab
So your Lord poured upon them a scourge of punishment.
14
إِنَّ رَبَّكَ لَبِالْمِرْصَادِ
Inna rabbaka la-bi-l-mirsad
Indeed, your Lord is in observation.
15
فَأَمَّا الْإِنسَانُ إِذَا مَا ابْتَلَاهُ رَبُّهُ فَأَكْرَمَهُ وَنَعَّمَهُ فَيَقُولُ رَبِّي أَكْرَمَنِ
Fa-amma-l-insanu idha ma-btalahu rabbuhu fa-akramahu wa na''amahu fa-yaqulu rabbi akraman
As for man, when his Lord tries him and [thus] is generous to him and favors him, he says, "My Lord has honored me."
16
وَأَمَّا إِذَا مَا ابْتَلَاهُ فَقَدَرَ عَلَيْهِ رِزْقَهُ فَيَقُولُ رَبِّي أَهَانَنِ
Wa amma idha ma-btalahu fa-qadara 'alayhi rizqahu fa-yaqulu rabbi ahanan
But as for man, when his Lord tries him and restricts his provision, he says, "My Lord has humiliated me."
17
كَلَّا ۖ بَل لَّا تُكْرِمُونَ الْيَتِيمَ
Kalla bal la tukrimuna-l-yatim
No! But you do not honor the orphan
18
وَلَا تَحَاضُّونَ عَلَىٰ طَعَامِ الْمِسْكِينِ
Wa la tahadduna 'ala ta'ami-l-miskin
And you do not encourage one another to feed the poor.
19
وَتَأْكُلُونَ التُّرَاثَ أَكْلًا لَّمًّا
Wa ta'kuluna-t-turatha aklan lamma
And you consume inheritance, devouring [it] altogether,
20
وَتُحِبُّونَ الْمَالَ حُبًّا جَمًّا
Wa tuhibbuna-l-mala hubban jamma
And you love wealth with immense love.
21
كَلَّا إِذَا دُكَّتِ الْأَرْضُ دَكًّا دَكًّا
Kalla idha dukkat-i-l-ardu dakkan dakka
No! When the earth has been leveled - pounded and crushed -
22
وَجَاءَ رَبُّكَ وَالْمَلَكُ صَفًّا صَفًّا
Wa ja'a rabbuka wa-l-malaku saffan saffa
And your Lord has come and the angels, rank upon rank,
23
وَجِيءَ يَوْمَئِذٍ بِجَهَنَّمَ ۚ يَوْمَئِذٍ يَتَذَكَّرُ الْإِنسَانُ وَأَنَّىٰ لَهُ الذِّكْرَىٰ
Wa ji'a yawma'idhin bi-jahannam yawma'idhin yatadhakkaru-l-insanu wa anna lahu-dh-dhikra
And brought [within view], that Day, is Hell - that Day, man will remember, but what good to him will be the remembrance?
24
يَقُولُ يَا لَيْتَنِي قَدَّمْتُ لِحَيَاتِي
Yaqulu ya laytani qaddamtu li-hayati
He will say, "Oh, I wish I had sent ahead [some good] for my life."
25
فَيَوْمَئِذٍ لَّا يُعَذِّبُ عَذَابَهُ أَحَدٌ
Fa-yawma'idhin la yu'adhibu 'adhabahu ahad
So on that Day, none will punish [as severely] as His punishment,
26
وَلَا يُوثِقُ وَثَاقَهُ أَحَدٌ
Wa la yuthiqu wathaqahu ahad
And none will bind [as severely] as His binding [of the evildoers].
27
يَا أَيَّتُهَا النَّفْسُ الْمُطْمَئِنَّةُ
Ya ayyatuha-n-nafsu-l-mutma'innah
[To the righteous it will be said], "O reassured soul,
28
ارْجِعِي إِلَىٰ رَبِّكِ رَاضِيَةً مَّرْضِيَّةً
Irji'i ila rabbiki radiya-tan mardiyyah
Return to your Lord, well-pleased and pleasing [to Him],
29
فَادْخُلِي فِي عِبَادِي
Fa-dkhuli fi 'ibadi
And enter among My [righteous] servants
30
وَادْخُلِي جَنَّتِي
Wa-dkhuli jannati
And enter My Paradise."

سُورَةُ الشَّمْس

Ash-Shams (The Sun)

Meccan 15 Verses
بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ
Bismillah-ir-Rahman-ir-Raheem
In the name of Allah, the Entirely Merciful, the Especially Merciful.
1
وَالشَّمْسِ وَضُحَاهَا
Wa-sh-shamsi wa duhaha
By the sun and its brightness
2
وَالْقَمَرِ إِذَا تَلَاهَا
Wa-l-qamari idha talaha
And [by] the moon when it follows it
3
وَالنَّهَارِ إِذَا جَلَّاهَا
Wa-n-nahari idha jallaha
And [by] the day when it displays it
4
وَاللَّيْلِ إِذَا يَغْشَاهَا
Wa-l-layli idha yaghshaha
And [by] the night when it covers it
5
وَالسَّمَاءِ وَمَا بَنَاهَا
Wa-s-sama'i wa ma banaha
And [by] the sky and He who constructed it
6
وَالْأَرْضِ وَمَا طَحَاهَا
Wa-l-ardi wa ma tahaha
And [by] the earth and He who spread it
7
وَنَفْسٍ وَمَا سَوَّاهَا
Wa nafsin wa ma sawwaha
And [by] the soul and He who proportioned it
8
فَأَلْهَمَهَا فُجُورَهَا وَتَقْوَاهَا
Fa-alhamaha fujuraha wa taqwaha
And inspired it [with discernment of] its wickedness and its righteousness,
9
قَدْ أَفْلَحَ مَن زَكَّاهَا
Qad aflaha man zakkaha
He has certainly succeeded who purifies it,
10
وَقَدْ خَابَ مَن دَسَّاهَا
Wa qad khaba man dassaha
And he has certainly failed who instills it [with corruption].
11
كَذَّبَتْ ثَمُودُ بِطَغْوَاهَا
Kadhabat thamudu bi-taghwaha
Thamud denied [the truth] out of arrogance
12
إِذِ انبَعَثَ أَشْقَاهَا
Idhi-nba'atha ashqaha
When the most wretched of them was sent forth.
13
فَقَالَ لَهُمْ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ نَاقَةَ اللَّهِ وَسُقْيَاهَا
Fa-qala lahum rasulu-llahi naqata-llahi wa suqyaha
And the messenger of Allah [Salih] said to them, "[Do not harm] the she-camel of Allah or [prevent her from] her drink."
14
فَكَذَّبُوهُ فَعَقَرُوهَا فَدَمْدَمَ عَلَيْهِمْ رَبُّهُم بِذَنبِهِمْ فَسَوَّاهَا
Fa-kadhabuhu fa-'aqaruha fa-damdam 'alayhim rabbuhum bi-dhanbihim fa-sawwaha
But they denied him and hamstrung her. So their Lord brought down upon them destruction for their sin and made it equal [upon all of them].
15
وَلَا يَخَافُ عُقْبَاهَا
Wa la yakhafu 'uqbaha
And He does not fear the consequence thereof.
