About the Holy Quran

The Quran is the holy book of Islam, believed by Muslims to be the direct word of Allah (God) as revealed to the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) through the angel Gabriel (Jibril). It serves as the ultimate source of guidance for all aspects of life.

"هُدًى لِّلْمُتَّقِين"
"A guidance for those conscious of Allah" - Quran 2:2

Quran Structure & Organization

The Quran is organized into 114 chapters (Surahs) of varying lengths, revealed over 23 years. Each surah is divided into verses (Ayahs) and contains guidance for all aspects of life.

Revelation Periods

Revelation Periods

Meccan Period (610-622 CE)

86 Surahs - Focus on faith, monotheism, and moral guidance

Key Themes: Oneness of Allah, Day of Judgment, moral conduct

Style: Shorter, rhythmic verses with powerful imagery

Medinan Period (622-632 CE)

28 Surahs - Focus on law, community, and social justice

Key Themes: Islamic law, community building, warfare

Style: Longer verses with detailed legal guidance
