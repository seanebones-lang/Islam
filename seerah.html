Seerah - Prophet's Biography

The Life Story of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ

What is Seerah?

Seerah is the study of the life, character, and teachings of Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him). It provides a comprehensive account of his birth, early life, prophethood, migration, and the establishment of the Islamic community. Studying Seerah helps us understand how to apply Islamic principles in our daily lives.

"وَمَا أَرْسَلْنَاكَ إِلَّا رَحْمَةً لِّلْعَالَمِينَ"
"And We have not sent you, [O Muhammad], except as a mercy to the worlds" - Quran 21:107

Key Periods of the Prophet's Life

Early Life (570-610 CE)

Birth in Mecca, childhood with Halima, youth as a merchant, marriage to Khadijah

Prophethood in Mecca (610-622 CE)

First revelation, early converts, persecution, the Night Journey, migration to Medina

Medina Period (622-632 CE)

Building the Islamic community, major battles, conquest of Mecca, final years

Early Life & Childhood

570 CE

Birth in Mecca

Born on Monday, 12th of Rabi' al-Awwal in the Year of the Elephant. His father Abdullah had passed away before his birth. (Sahih Bukhari 3549)

576 CE

Mother's Death

His mother Amina passed away when he was 6 years old. He was then cared for by his grandfather Abdul-Muttalib. (Sahih Bukhari 3549)

578 CE

Grandfather's Death

His grandfather Abdul-Muttalib passed away when he was 8. He was then cared for by his uncle Abu Talib. (Sahih Bukhari 3549)

582 CE

Shepherd Boy

He worked as a shepherd in his youth, which taught him patience and responsibility. (Sahih Bukhari 2262)

595 CE

Marriage to Khadijah

Married Khadijah bint Khuwaylid at age 25. She was 40 years old and a successful businesswoman. (Sahih Bukhari 3)

Prophethood & Revelation

The First Revelation (610 CE)

اقْرَأْ بِاسْمِ رَبِّكَ الَّذِي خَلَقَ

"Read in the name of your Lord who created" - Quran 96:1

At age 40, while meditating in Cave Hira, the Angel Gabriel appeared and commanded him to read. This marked the beginning of his prophethood. (Sahih Bukhari 3)

Early Years of Prophethood

For 3 years, he preached secretly to close family and friends. The first to accept Islam were Khadijah, Ali, Zayd ibn Haritha, and Abu Bakr. (Sahih Bukhari 3)

Public Preaching (613 CE)

After 3 years, Allah commanded him to preach publicly. He faced increasing persecution from the Quraysh leaders. (Sahih Bukhari 3)

The Hijra (Migration) to Medina

Planning the Migration (622 CE)

After 13 years of persecution in Mecca, Allah permitted the Prophet to migrate to Medina. He and Abu Bakr secretly left Mecca and hid in Cave Thawr for 3 days. (Sahih Bukhari 3905)

The Journey to Medina

They traveled for 8 days to reach Medina, where the Ansar (Helpers) welcomed them with great joy. This marked the beginning of the Islamic calendar. (Sahih Bukhari 3905)

Building the Islamic State

In Medina, he established the first Islamic state, built the first mosque, and created the Constitution of Medina - the first written constitution in history. (Sahih Bukhari 1)

Major Battles & Events

Battle of Badr (624 CE)

313 Muslims vs 1000 Quraysh

The first major battle where Muslims achieved victory against overwhelming odds. This established the foundation of the Islamic state. (Sahih Bukhari 3953)

Battle of Uhud (625 CE)

700 Muslims vs 3000 Quraysh

A difficult battle where Muslims faced defeat due to disobedience, but learned important lessons about unity and discipline. (Sahih Bukhari 4043)

Battle of the Trench (627 CE)

3000 Muslims vs 10,000 Allies

Muslims dug a trench around Medina to defend against a massive coalition. The siege lasted 27 days before the enemy retreated. (Sahih Bukhari 4110)

Conquest of Mecca (630 CE)

10,000 Muslims marched peacefully

The Prophet returned to Mecca with 10,000 followers and conquered it without bloodshed, showing mercy to all his former enemies. (Sahih Bukhari 4287)

Prophet's Family and Companions

Wives (Mothers of the Believers)

Khadijah bint Khuwaylid

First wife, married for 25 years. She was the first to believe in his prophethood and supported him throughout the difficult early years. (Sahih Bukhari 3)

Aisha bint Abu Bakr

Daughter of Abu Bakr, married at age 9. Known for her intelligence and knowledge, she narrated over 2,000 hadiths. (Sahih Bukhari 3894)

Sawda bint Zam'a

Widow who married the Prophet after Khadijah's death. Known for her piety and devotion. (Sahih Bukhari 3894)

Hafsa bint Umar

Daughter of Umar ibn al-Khattab. The Quran was compiled during her time as custodian. (Sahih Bukhari 3894)

Children

Sons (All died in childhood)

Qasim, Abdullah (also called Tayyib and Tahir), and Ibrahim. The Prophet said, "I am the father of the poor and the poor are my family." (Sahih Bukhari 5374)

Daughters

Zaynab, Ruqayyah, Umm Kulthum, and Fatimah. Fatimah was his favorite and is known as "Sayyidat Nisa al-Jannah" (Leader of the Women of Paradise). (Sahih Bukhari 6282)

The Rightly Guided Companions

Abu Bakr as-Siddiq

First Caliph, closest friend, and first adult male to accept Islam. Known for his truthfulness and wisdom. (Sahih Bukhari 5)

Umar ibn al-Khattab

Second Caliph, known as "Al-Farooq" (the distinguisher). His conversion strengthened Islam. (Sahih Bukhari 3684)

Uthman ibn Affan

Third Caliph, known for his generosity and modesty. He compiled the Quran into one book. (Sahih Bukhari 3704)

Ali ibn Abi Talib

Fourth Caliph, cousin and son-in-law of the Prophet. Known for his courage and knowledge. (Sahih Bukhari 3704)

Bilal ibn Rabah

Former slave who became the first mu'adhin (caller to prayer). Known for his beautiful voice and steadfastness. (Sahih Bukhari 604)

Khabab ibn al-Aratt

Early convert who endured severe torture but never renounced his faith. (Sahih Bukhari 3852)

Miracles and Signs

The Night Journey (Isra & Mi'raj)

In 621 CE, the Prophet was taken from Mecca to Jerusalem and then to the heavens in one night. This miraculous journey is mentioned in the Quran and confirmed by authentic hadiths. (Sahih Bukhari 3207)

Splitting of the Moon

The Prophet split the moon into two parts as a sign for the disbelievers of Mecca. This miracle is mentioned in the Quran: "The Hour has come near, and the moon has been cleft asunder." (Quran 54:1)

Water from Fingers

During the Battle of Badr, when the Muslims were thirsty, the Prophet placed his fingers in a water container and water flowed from between his fingers, satisfying all 300+ companions. (Sahih Bukhari 3576)

Multiplication of Food

On several occasions, small amounts of food multiplied to feed large numbers of people. Once, a small amount of barley and dates fed 80 people. (Sahih Bukhari 3576)

Healing the Sick

The Prophet healed many people through his prayers and touch. He cured blindness, paralysis, and other ailments by the permission of Allah. (Sahih Bukhari 5736)

Knowledge of the Unseen

Allah revealed to him many future events, including the conquest of Persia and Rome, and the fate of various individuals. Many of these prophecies have already come true. (Sahih Bukhari 3593)

Lessons from the Prophet's Life

Mercy and Compassion

The Prophet showed mercy to all, including his enemies, teaching us to be compassionate in all situations.

Justice and Fairness

He established justice for all, regardless of social status, race, or background.

Importance of Knowledge

He emphasized learning and education, saying "Seek knowledge from the cradle to the grave."

Community Building

He created a strong, united community based on brotherhood, mutual support, and shared values.
